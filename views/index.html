The JikoPower Team

OUR TEAM MEMBERS

JikoPower is a diverse team completely made up of full-time University of Washington students who possess the skills, experience, and, most importantly, passion necessary to refine and market the JikoPower Personal Power Station.

Ryan Ahearn

Senior Mechanical Engineering undergraduate at UW

Ryan is project leader for UW EcoCAR, a proud father, and a skilled machinist. He is currently involved with Engineers without Borders, working to bring technology education to the people of Uganda. Ryan spent four years managing an entertainment venue prior to attending the UW.

Aaron Owen

Senior Mechanical Engineering undergraduate at UW

Aaron Owen is an undergraduate research assistant in the UW Microtechnology Lab where he is working on another clean energy generator involving magnetic levitation. Aaron will be making his second trip to Africa this year as part of a UW program on "ecopreneurship" in developing countries

Daniel Parrish

Senior Mechanical Engineering undergraduate at UW

Daniel Parrish is a master mechanic and has been working with heating problems in electronics for years before joining JikoPower.

Joe Koch

Foster School of Business MBA student

Joe Koch is a Foster School of Business MBA student with a passion for content, design, and innovative products. He has provided management consulting to several Fortune 500 companies in the area of process improvement. He has also worked with two startup companies to provide brand strategy, design and marketing consulting services.

Meghna Singla

Foster School of Business

Meghna Singla worked with Google for seven years prior to coming to Foster School of Business, where she gained significant experience in cross-functional leadership, project management, enterprise sales, digital marketing and client and vendor relationship management. Her passion for innovation and technology got her to Google and now to Seattle. She plans to continue her career in the tech industry focusing on Marketing and Product Management side of business.

Shubha Pratiwadibhayankar

MBA student at the Foster School of Business,

Shubha Pratiwadibhayankar is an MBA student at the Foster School of Business, and a Senior Program Manager at Microsoft. Shubha grew up in India, and is all too familiar with the problems associated with the lack of electricity in developing countries.
