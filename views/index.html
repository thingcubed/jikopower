POWERING DEVICES. EMPOWERING PEOPLE

A personal power generator that turns excess heat from cook fires into electricity for homes across Africa and the world.

Project Spec

The Core Problem

The Problem

One out of three Africans owns a mobile phone, but over 135 million in Sub-Saharan Africa cannot charge their phones at home due to lack of electricity.

Solutions

The Solutions

JikoPower provides an electrical charging solution right in the home, putting power directly into the hands of the consumer. It does this by converting heat energy from a cook stove into electricity to charge cell phones and LED lamps.

OUR TEAM MEMBERS

JikoPower is a diverse team completely made up of full-time University of Washington students who possess the skills, experience, and, most importantly, passion necessary to refine and market the JikoPower Personal Power Station.

Ryan Ahearn

Senior Mechanical Engineering undergraduate at UW

Ryan is project leader for UW EcoCAR, a proud father, and a skilled machinist. He is currently involved with Engineers without Borders, working to bring technology education to the people of Uganda. Ryan spent four years managing an entertainment venue prior to attending the UW.

Aaron Owen

Aaron Owen is an undergraduate research assistant in the UW Microtechnology Lab where he is working on another clean energy generator involving magnetic levitation. Aaron will be making his second trip to Africa this year as part of a UW program on "ecopreneurship" in developing countries
